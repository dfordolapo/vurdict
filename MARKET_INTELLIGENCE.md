# Vurdict Market Intelligence

This document outlines the strategic positioning of Vurdict within the designer portfolio review and AI feedback landscape.

---

## 1. Problem Landscape
Product designers face a "feedback bottleneck." After spending months on a portfolio, they often send it into a void.
- **Recruiter Reality**: Portfolios are scanned for 30-60 seconds. If the "signals" (process, impact, niche) aren't immediate, the designer is rejected.
- **Feedback Quality**: Existing feedback is either too nice (friends), too generic (standard ChatGPT), or too slow (mentorship bookings).
- **Context Gap**: Most reviewers don't ask: "What is your goal?" A portfolio for a FAANG job should look fundamentally different from one for a freelance brand identity gig.

## 2. Current User Behavior
- **The Browser Refresh**: Designers constantly tweak their sites and check for LinkedIn profile views, guessing what's wrong.
- **Mentorship Roulette**: Booking free sessions on ADPList. Results vary wildly based on the mentor's mood and specific background.
- **AI Prompting**: Using ChatGPT/Claude to "review my text" without the AI actually seeing the visual layout or understanding the "signals" hiring managers look for.

## 3. Competitor Analysis

| Competitor Type | Examples | Strengths | Weaknesses |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Portfolio Builders** | UXfolio, Behance | Integrated templates; easy to use. | Passive feedback; "Job Fit" checkers are often surface-level. |
| **Human Mentorship** | ADPList, MentorCruise | High empathy; deep industry nuance. | Slow (async or booked); inconsistent; bias-heavy. |
| **Figma Plugins** | AI Design Reviewer | Checks UI/Accessibility in real-time. | Zero strategy/storytelling analysis; focused on screens, not careers. |
| **Generic AI** | ChatGPT Plus, Claude | Fast; helps with writing. | No specific design rubric; lacks "Senior Design Lead" persona calibration. |

## 4. Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)
- **Validation**: "Is my portfolio actually good enough to get interviews?"
- **Prioritization**: "I have 5 hours this weekend. What is the *one* thing I should fix to have the most impact?"
- **Confidence**: "I want to stop second-guessing my layout and know it meets professional standards."

## 5. Market Gaps
- **Goal-Aware Spock**: There is no tool that evaluates a portfolio through two distinct lenses (Employment vs. Freelance) using a standardized, professional rubric.
- **The "Audit" Experience**: Most tools are for *creating*. Vurdict is for *auditing*. It’s a second pair of expert eyes, not a paintbrush.

## 6. Positioning Statement
Vurdict is the **objective audit layer** for product designers who need high-fidelity, goal-aware feedback *before* they hit "Apply." We bridge the gap between "done" and "hired" by providing the brutal honesty of a Senior Design Lead in under 60 seconds.

## 7. Key Differentiators
- **High-Fidelity AI Role**: We don't just "summarize." We act as a Senior Design Lead calibrated by a professional rubric.
- **Dual-Lens Evaluation**: Dynamic weighting of categories based on whether the user wants a full-time role or a freelance client.
- **The "Fix This First" Engine**: Rather than a list of 50 things, we identify the single highest-priority bottleneck.
- **Brutal Honesty**: Vurdict is designed to be "Spock-like"—objective and data-driven, avoiding the "toxic positivity" of community feedback.

## 8. Risks and Assumptions
- **Extraction Fidelity**: *Assumption*: Jina Reader can effectively parse enough structural information from a URL to "understand" the story. *Risk*: Highly visual/Flash-heavy portfolios might be hard to read.
- **Hallucination**: *Risk*: AI might "imagine" results or impact that aren't there. *Mitigation*: Strict grounding in the `RUBRIC.md`.
- **Value Perception**: *Risk*: Users might find the feedback too "harsh." *Mitigation*: Positioning it as "The Audit You Need, Not the Praise You Want."
